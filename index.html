What is Encryption?

Encryption, by definition, is "the process of converting information or data into a code, especially to prevent unauthorized access".

What is Decryption?

Decryption, by definition, is the opposite of encryption, which is the conversion of encrypted data back into its original form.



Caesar encryption is one of the earliest and simplest of encryption methods. The recipient is given the cipher and uses the key to decrypt the message, which is usually a "left-shift" of 3. For example, the letter "D" would be decrypted as "A", and you would encrypt "B" as "E". For example, if the plaintext were
"HELLO", the resulting message would be:
"KHOOR"

Rotational cryptography is a bit similar to Caesar. It involves a key that simply translates the plaintext into another based on the numbers given. For example, if the plaintext were
"HELLO", with the keys
"2 2 4 4 2", the resulting message would be:
"JGPPQ"

Pigpen encryption is a bit more complicated than the previous two. It involves a system of grids, dots, and in some cases, x's. In order to decypher the message, one must find the letter corresponding to the letter in the given grid, using the dots to determine which grid to look at, and the shape of the symbol to figure out which letter to choose.
